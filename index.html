Correlations Between Wealth and Health

The graph above allows us to toggle between different factors indicating wealth (Household Income and Poverty rate) and health (Percent Smokers and Obese) in each state.

Each combination clearly shows that the wealthier a state is, the better health behaviors it has. There is a clear negative correlation between income and obesity and smoking rates. Meanwhile, there is a clear positive correlation between poverty rate and the rate of obesity and smokers.

It is likely that this correlation stems from the access to high quality education and nutrional information for the richer communities, as well as the ability to purchase higher quality products.
